Final Portfolio Project: Movement, Rhythm, Montage and Sound

This project explores movement and rhythm through original video montage, enhanced with sound and music. Your work should focus on one visual component: space, line, shape, tone, or color, and present a rhythmic editing structure that integrates environmental sound details and a music track.

To complete the assignment, produce an original video that is at least 3 minutes long, upload it to YouTube or Vimeo, then submit the link in Canvas. Also include an artist statement of at least 250 words explaining your concept, sound choices, and the effect you aimed to achieve.

Project requirements

  • Original video capture with a focus on movement and rhythm.
  • Use sound and/or music to intensify the montage structure.
  • Choose one visual component to emphasize: space, line, shape, tone, or color.
  • Project length must be at least 3 minutes.
  • Upload final work to YouTube or Vimeo.
  • Submit the video link and your artist statement in Canvas.
  • Provide feedback on at least two classmates’ projects by July 3rd.

Artist statement guidance

In your artist statement, explain the concept behind your montage, how your sound choices support the visuals, and what rhythm or movement strategies you used in editing. Describe how the chosen visual component is expressed through your footage and how the audio and image work together to create the final effect.

Be specific about transitions between environmental sound details and how the music track complements the visual pacing. Explain why the chosen editing decisions help the viewer understand the movement, rhythm, and narrative flow of your piece.

Submission checklist

  1. Capture original video footage.
  2. Edit a montage with sound and music.
  3. Upload to YouTube or Vimeo.
  4. Write a 250+ word artist statement.
  5. Submit the link and statement in Canvas.
  6. Give feedback to at least two other students.
