Final Portfolio Project: Movement, Rhythm, Montage and Sound

Artist Statement

For my final portfolio project, I wanted to create a montage that treats movement as both a visual subject and a rhythmic structure. The piece is built from original footage of people, objects, and spaces in motion, then arranged so that each shot feels like part of a larger pulse. I focused especially on the visual component of line, using paths, edges, repeated directions, and changes in screen movement to guide the viewer through the sequence. Some shots emphasize direct physical movement, while others use camera motion, framing, or editing rhythm to make still environments feel active.

Sound is central to the concept. I used environmental sound details as transitions between visual ideas, so the montage does not depend only on image order. Footsteps, motion sounds, ambient textures, and small changes in volume help connect one moment to the next. The music track gives the project a steady rhythmic foundation, but I wanted the natural sounds to keep the piece grounded in real spaces. Instead of using sound only as background, I tried to make it function like an editing tool: it introduces momentum, marks changes in pace, and intensifies the relationship between separate images.

The overall effect I aimed for was a layered visual narrative where individual shots become more meaningful through montage. Chapter 18's discussion of complex editing helped me think about how meaning can be created through relationships between images rather than through a single continuous action. By combining movement, rhythm, line, music, and environmental sound, I wanted the final project to feel like a structured experience of attention: the viewer is carried from one visual beat to the next while the sound design shapes the emotional pace.
